Grammar: "Will" (Promises)

We use the word WILL to talk about the future and to make promises to people.

Promise 1

"I will make you queen."

He promises to treat her like a queen in the future.

Promise 2

"I will buy you a house."

He promises to give her a house of gold.

Fact vs. Promise

See how adding "will" changes the sentence from a simple fact today to a promise for tomorrow.

Click the button to change the perspective

I help you. (Fact: I do this every day.)
I will help you. (Promise: I promise to do this in the future.)

Formula: Person + will + Action (Verb)

How do you say "NO" to the future?

Spoken

"I'll"

In conversation, native speakers almost always say "I'll" instead of "I will". It sounds exactly like the word "aisle".
